Task 1: Review professional writing characteristics

In this task, I have read and considered the following professional writing characteristics:

  • Accuracy
  • Active Voice
  • Limit Bias
  • Clarity
  • Conciseness
  • Conversational and smoothly flowing prose
  • Correctness
  • Parallelism
  • Positive voice
  • Strong nouns and verbs
  • Sentence Variety
  • Simple Words
  • Shorter Paragraphs
  • Tone and voice

Reviewing them helped me realise the ways I can successfully deliver my messages to the website users.

Task 2: Evaluate resources

For this task, I have selected the Flux Academy’s website. It is a website that has a similar purpose to my website - to educate designers on how to effectively build websites. Here’s a link to the website.

Evaluating Flux Academy’s website against the fourteen professional writing characteristics:

  • The website’s content is accurate. The text is well-written and created with attention to detail.
  • Active voice is used throughout the website.
  • I cannot spot any limit biases within the website. No age bias, racial bias, or implicit bias can be noticed.
  • The words used by the content creators are correctly used and can be recognised by the target audience - designers.
  • Wordiness is avoided.
  • The used language is simple. The sentences are not complex, they are easy to follow.
  • I cannot spot any grammar errors, or words that are used poorly.
  • Parallelism is used from time to time.
  • A positive voice is used.
  • The website offers a service. The nouns and verbs that are used are strong. They successfully do the job of selling the product.
  • The sentences vary in sentence length combining short, medium and longer sentences.
  • People from all around the globe access the website, and this is why recognisable words over showy words are used.
  • We can notice short paragraphs that can easily keep the user’s attention.
  • Correct tone and voice are used at all times.

Task 3: The Awe writing process

Another thing that I will take into consideration when creating my website’s content is the Awe writing process. Three are the steps I need to follow. The first step is to Assess. This includes defining the audiences, defining the purpose, conducting research, which I already did, and organising, categorising & and outlining. The second step is to write - creating a draft and reading and revising. The last step is to edit my text. It involves checking for correctness and organisation and editing paragraphs and sentence levels.
